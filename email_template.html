Society Updates:
CyberSoc
This week we'll be starting off with some elections to elect people into the vacant positions on our committee. We'll then switch back to our usual format for a talk on stenography - the practice of obscuring data! Join us on Wednesday at 7pm in PZA/106X to look at how you can hide files in files, hide files after files and hide messages in the data of those files.
YorDevs
Did you know HTML had graphics drawing functionality? I didn't.
Join us this week as we'll be exploring the power of HTML Canvas by making a simple web-based game. We'll use HTML, CSS and Javascript to create our masterpiece so no paint required!
HackSoc
On Wednesday at 12:00 we have our weekly lunch event. Come join us in Piazza, have lunch together and chat about whatever you feel like!
On Friday we also have our traditional board games and cake event at 19:00. Come join us in the pod (CSE/80) and play board games with us. Feel free to bring your own board games if you want.
AI Society
This week, we will be running a tutorial session on Neural Networks and Convolutional Neural Networks. Our goal is to build an image recognition system, so we can give our network an image and it will tell us what it thinks it is!
Thursday 6pm in G/N/169
DevSoc
We've got our second game jam of the year coming up! It will be another week long jam from Friday 22nd - Friday 29th. We will have a short Jackbox Games social at 7pm on the evening of the first Friday after which we will announce the details of the jam such as the theme. We will gather in the evening on the final Friday to showcase the games we have made. We will be using the room PZA/023 each Friday and there will be a DevScript on the Wednesday (27th) in the middle where people can catch up on their progress.

Any questions, reach out on the DevSoc Discord. I'm aware a lot of people haven't done game jams before. It's a really fun and casual way to get into game development so please get stuck in if it interests you. The rules are simple: you have one week to make a game based on a theme, using whatever method / assets you want. There's no competition or pressure to make a masterpiece (most people's games are just unfinished prototypes).
