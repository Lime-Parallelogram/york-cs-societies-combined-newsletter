This week we'll be exploring Smart Home & IOT through a lense of openness and security. Our talk this week will present a model for a private and secure Smart Home and contrast it with the more conventional offerings - the dumb smart home if you will.

We'll be demystifying terms like Zigbee and Matter, exploring the ultimate open and maintainable solution (ESPHome) and asking the question: where is the red line for things that just shouldn't be connected to the internet? The sauce we'll be opening this week will be a tomato salsa with nachos!

No events this week but we've already announced details of our first Game Jam for 2025, beginning on Monday 17th March @ 19:00 in PZA/023. This will be a week long jam starting on the 17th and ending on the 24th. On the first Monday we will play some Jackbox games then vote on a theme for the jam (and people can form teams if they want). On the 24th we will do the Game Jam Showcase where people can showcase what they've made.

In addition, mark your calendars for next week: we're excited to announce that we'll be doing a Unity Workshop on Tuesday 18th March 5:30-7:30 in the Creativity Lab (Fairhurst Library). It will be focused on 3D level design and it's being run by Ross Fifield who has lots of experience teaching game development. This is aimed at beginners/intermediate game developers. You don't need to have used Unity before but we do ask that you have some experience with game dev. It will take place in a computer lab which has PCs with Unity installed but you can bring your own laptops if you want to.

Finally, we will be running our AGM before the Game Jam Showcase (24th). We'd love to see as many people there as possible whether they are taking part in the jam or not.
